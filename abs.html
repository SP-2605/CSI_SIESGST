Abstract Submission

Abstracts must include sufficient information for reviewers to judge the nature and significance of the topic, the adequacy of the investigative strategy, the nature of the results, and the conclusions. The abstract should summarize the substantive results of the work and not merely list topics to be discussed.
An abstract is an outline/brief summary of your paper and your whole project. It should have an intro, body and conclusion. It is a well-developed paragraph, should be exact in wording, and must be understandable to a wide audience. Abstracts should be no more than 250 words, formatted in Microsoft Word, and single-spaced, using size 12 and Times New Roman font.
If your title includes scientific notation, Greek letters, bold, italics, or other special characters/symbols, do make sure they appear correctly.
Here is the sample abstract format for submission. Please follow the format carefully. There will be 2 rounds of abstract evaluation. Only the final outcome will be conveyed to you. You shall be notified of your selections as we evaluate your abstracts and there is no timeline for the same. Initial preference of abstract evaluation will be given to those participants who do not reside in Maharashtra so that they can look into their travel arrangements and accommodations.

Format

    Presentation Title: Title given for the project
    Research Focus: (Area/Domain)
    School/College: Mention the full name of your college, avoid using abbreveations.
    Team Leader: Name of team leader as filled in google forms
    Team Members: Names of team members seperated by ','
    Description (100 to 250 words): Description must contain a general idea of the project and must contain technical details along with images,flowcharts that may provide better understanding of the project.
    Technical Requirements: Mention if there are any on day requirements that you expect from the college such as 3 phase power supply.
